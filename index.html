The Great American Who..?

William Faulkner is considered one of the progenitors of The Great American Novels - canonical works that are thought to embody the essence of that nation, generally dealing in some way with the question of its character.

However, Faulkner's greatest asset is the ability to transcend those boundaries. His works speak on the human race as one, inherently flawed, but fascinating mechanism of contradiction and confoundment.

Master of Modernism

Faulkner was one of the most skilled exponents of literary modernism - a movement characterized by a self-conscious break with traditional ways of storytelling. He experimented greatly with literary form and expression.

In Faulkner's prose one can experience:

  • non-linear narratives
  • free-flowing interior monologues
  • unreliable narrators
  • intricate wordplay and atypical narrative forms

Visit a place that doesn't exist!

Yoknapatawpha County (/jɒknəpə'tɔ:fə/) is a fictional Mississippi county created by Faulkner to set the majority of his novels and short stories in. It is one of the most intricate literary universes ever created, with a consistent geography, detailed history, family genealogies and even linguistic quirks and idiolects. This 'Faulknerverse' connects characters and events over time and space, even if they themselves don't overlap.
